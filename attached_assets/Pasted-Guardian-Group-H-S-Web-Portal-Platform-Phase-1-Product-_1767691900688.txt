Guardian Group – H&S Web Portal Platform
Phase 1 Product Brief & Developer RFP
Document Type: Developer RFP / Product Definition
Version: 1.0
Prepared by: Guardian Group
Purpose: To scope, design, and deliver Phase 1 of a consultancy-led compliance web portal
1. Project Overview
Guardian Group is seeking a technology partner to design and build Phase 1 of a secure, scalable H&S web portal that supports a hybrid consultancy + SaaS compliance delivery model.
This platform will:
Digitally support Guardian Group’s professional services
Improve compliance visibility and auditability for clients
Reduce internal administrative burden
Create a scalable foundation for future functionality (mobile app, AI, integrations)
This is not a standalone, self-serve SaaS platform.
Clients will not generate compliance content themselves. Guardian Group remains the author, controller, and authority of compliance outputs.
2. Objectives
Primary objectives
Centralise compliance documentation and evidence
Enable digital review and approval workflows
Create a robust audit trail for all compliance activity
Improve operational efficiency for consultants and admin teams
Secondary objectives
Improve client experience and engagement
Increase retention and long-term client value
Support Guardian Group’s unified brand and service model
3. Target Users & Roles
User types
Clients
Consultants / Advisors
Internal Guardian Group administrators
Role-based access
Role-based permissions (RBAC) required
Different visibility and actions by role
Certain roles must be able to approve documents
All actions must be logged with timestamp and user attribution
4. Platform Structure & Scope
4.1 Modular architecture
The platform must support modular functionality aligned to client entitlements.
Initial verticals
Health & Safety
HR / Employment Law
Each vertical will:
Be switchable per client
Contain sub-modules depending on commercial package
Be extensible for future verticals
4.2 Multi-entity support
The platform must support:
Multiple legal entities per client
Multiple sites per entity
Clear separation of documents and compliance status by site/entity
5. Phase 1 Functional Scope (In Scope)
5.1 Central Document Management
The core of Phase 1 is a central compliance document store with full lifecycle management.
Required capabilities:
Secure document storage
Version control and document history
Archived documents retained and accessible
Guardian Group uploads and maintains documents
Clients can view and approve documents digitally
Clear approval status indicators
Full audit trail of:
Uploads
Changes
Approvals
Access
5.2 Supported Deliverables
The platform must support storage and management of:
Policies
Risk assessments
Audits
Assessment outputs
Compliance documentation
Incident logs
Checklists and templates (client-completed only)
5.3 Forms, Logs & Templates
Guardian Group creates and controls all templates
Clients may:
Complete assigned forms and checklists
Upload supporting evidence
Clients may not create templates or compliance content
5.4 Compliance Status & Dashboards
High-level compliance dashboard per client
RAG (Red / Amber / Green) indicators for:
Document status
Review/expiry cycles
Outstanding items
Clear visibility of current compliance position
5.5 Automation & Notifications
Automated reminders for:
Document reviews
Expiry dates
Outstanding compliance actions
Notification logic must be configurable by Guardian Group
5.6 Reporting
Ability to generate and export reports (PDF / Word as minimum)
Reporting should reflect:
Compliance status
Document history
Audit trail
5.7 Support Requests
Clients must be able to request support or advice through the platform
Requests should be logged and auditable
6. Consultant Capabilities (Phase 1)
Consultants must be able to:
Upload and manage documents
Create and update assessments and outputs
Create action plans
Review client submissions
Explicitly excluded in Phase 1
Task assignment workflows
Scheduling, routing, or site visit planning
7. Out of Scope (Phase 1)
The following are explicitly excluded from Phase 1:
Asset registers
COSHH, RAMS, inspections
Full EHS task management
Daily operational management tools
HR/people management functionality
Client-generated compliance content
Third-party partner access
Dedicated mobile app
8. Mobile & Offline Requirements
Phase 1
Responsive, mobile-friendly web platform
No offline capability required
Future phases (for awareness only)
Dedicated mobile app for consultants
Offline capability for on-site assessments
Evidence capture and sync once online
The Phase 1 solution must be architected to allow this future expansion.
9. Integrations (Future Consideration)
No integrations are required for Phase 1 delivery, but the solution must be architected to support future integration with:
HRIS platforms
Training platforms
Safety schemes
CRM systems
Finance systems (invoice visibility)
10. Non-Functional Requirements
Security & Compliance
Secure authentication
Role-based access control
Data segregation between clients
Full audit logging
UK data hosting preferred
Scalability
Must support growth across multiple Guardian Group divisions
Must support increasing document volume and users
UX Principles
Consultant- and client-friendly
Clear, simple navigation
Compliance-first design (clarity over complexity)

Phase 1 will be considered successful if it delivers:
A stable, secure central compliance portal
Reduced manual document handling
Clear audit trails and approval workflows
Positive consultant and client adoption
